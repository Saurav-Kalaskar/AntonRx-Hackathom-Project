UnitedHealthcare Gastroenterology & Oncology Policy — Nivolumab (Opdivo) and Adalimumab (Humira)
Effective Date: January 2024 | Policy Number: UHC-GAST-ONC-2024-003

================================================================================
SECTION 1: NIVOLUMAB (OPDIVO) — ONCOLOGY POLICY
================================================================================

1.1 COVERED INDICATIONS
Nivolumab (Opdivo) is a PD-1 checkpoint inhibitor covered under UHC's medical benefit for:

- Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC):
  - First-line combination with ipilimumab (Yervoy) for metastatic NSCLC with PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1% and no EGFR/ALK alterations
  - First-line combination with ipilimumab plus 2 cycles of chemotherapy regardless of PD-L1 status
  - Second-line monotherapy for metastatic NSCLC with progression after platinum-based chemo

- Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC):
  - First-line combination with ipilimumab for advanced/metastatic clear-cell RCC (intermediate/poor risk per IMDC criteria)
  - First-line combination with cabozantinib for advanced RCC
  - Second-line monotherapy after prior anti-angiogenic therapy

- Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC):
  - Second-line monotherapy or combination with ipilimumab after prior sorafenib

- Gastric/GEJ Adenocarcinoma:
  - First-line combination with chemotherapy for HER2-negative, PD-L1 CPS ≥ 5 tumors

- Melanoma:
  - First-line monotherapy or combination with ipilimumab for unresectable/metastatic melanoma
  - Adjuvant therapy after complete resection of Stage IIB-IV melanoma

1.2 PRIOR AUTHORIZATION REQUIREMENTS — NIVOLUMAB
Required Documentation:
1. Pathology report with ICD-10 code confirming cancer type and stage
2. PD-L1 expression result (TPS for NSCLC, CPS for GEJ):
   - NSCLC monotherapy: PD-L1 TPS ≥ 1%
   - GEJ/Gastric: PD-L1 CPS ≥ 5 required for first-line combination
   - RCC and Melanoma: PD-L1 testing not required
3. EGFR and ALK mutation testing for NSCLC patients (must be negative for checkpoint therapy)
4. ECOG performance status (0-1); ECOG 2 requires additional clinical justification
5. For combination with ipilimumab: Documented absence of active autoimmune disease
6. Most recent CT/MRI imaging report confirming measurable disease per RECIST 1.1

Step Therapy for Opdivo NSCLC Second-Line:
- Patient must have received and progressed on at least one prior platinum-based doublet chemotherapy
- Documented disease progression per radiographic imaging

UHC BIOMARKER THRESHOLD POLICY — IMPORTANT:
UHC requires PD-L1 expression ≥ 50% (TPS) for single-agent checkpoint inhibitor use as
first-line therapy in NSCLC WITHOUT combination chemotherapy. Patients with PD-L1 TPS 1-49%
are required to receive pembrolizumab in combination with platinum-based chemotherapy or
nivolumab/ipilimumab combination therapy.

1.3 DOSING AND AUTHORIZATION DURATION
- Nivolumab monotherapy: 240 mg IV Q2W or 480 mg IV Q4W
- Nivolumab + Ipilimumab: Nivolumab 3 mg/kg Q3W + Ipilimumab 1 mg/kg Q3W (4 cycles), then Nivolumab 480 mg Q4W
- Maximum duration: 24 months for adjuvant; until progression or toxicity for metastatic
- Authorization period: 3 months (renewable)

SECTION 2: ADALIMUMAB (HUMIRA / BIOSIMILAR) — RHEUMATOLOGY & GI POLICY
================================================================================

2.1 COVERED INDICATIONS FOR ADALIMUMAB
- Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA): Moderate-to-severe (ICD-10: M05.x, M06.x)
- Psoriatic Arthritis (ICD-10: M07.3)
- Ankylosing Spondylitis (ICD-10: M45.x)
- Plaque Psoriasis: Moderate-to-severe (ICD-10: L40.0)
- Crohn's Disease: Moderate-to-severe (ICD-10: K50.x)
- Ulcerative Colitis: Moderate-to-severe (ICD-10: K51.x)
- Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA): Polyarticular (patients ≥ 2 years)

2.2 BIOSIMILAR SUBSTITUTION REQUIREMENT — CRITICAL POLICY
UHC mandates biosimilar-first prescribing for adalimumab effective January 1, 2024.

PREFERRED biosimilars (must be prescribed before brand Humira):
- Hadlima (adalimumab-bwwd) — citrate-free, high-concentration formulation
- Hyrimoz (adalimumab-adaz) — citrate-free formulation
- Yusimry (adalimumab-aqvh)

Brand Humira is covered ONLY if:
- Patient has documented intolerance to at least one preferred biosimilar AND
- Clinical justification is provided by the treating specialist

2.3 STEP THERAPY — RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS
Before adalimumab biosimilar is covered for RA, patient must have:
Step 1: Tried and failed (inadequate response ≥ 3 months, or intolerance) methotrexate (MTX):
   - Minimum dose: 15 mg/week, titrated to 20-25 mg/week if tolerated
   - If MTX contraindicated: Leflunomide (Arava) 20 mg/day for 3 months is acceptable alternative

Step 2: Only if biologic combination is requested: Documented MTX failure with option to add adalimumab

Exception criteria (bypass step therapy):
- Radiographic evidence of rapid joint destruction (erosive disease on X-ray within 6 months)
- CDAI > 28 or DAS28-CRP > 5.1 despite adequate MTX trial
- MTX contraindicated due to hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh A or B), renal impairment (CrCl < 30 mL/min), or pregnancy

2.4 DOSING FOR ADALIMUMAB / BIOSIMILARS
- RA, PsA, AS: 40 mg SC every other week (EOW)
- RA (monotherapy, high disease activity): 40 mg SC weekly
- Plaque Psoriasis: 80 mg SC at Week 0, 40 mg EOW starting Week 1
- Crohn's Disease: 160 mg SC Week 0, 80 mg Week 2, 40 mg EOW
- Ulcerative Colitis: 160 mg SC Week 0, 80 mg Week 2, 40 mg EOW

Pediatric dosing varies by weight — refer to package insert.

2.5 TUBERCULOSIS AND INFECTION SCREENING
All patients initiating adalimumab or biosimilar must have:
- QuantiFERON-TB Gold or TB skin test (TST) within 6 months prior to initiation
- Positive TB test requires 9 months of prophylactic isoniazid completed before biologic initiation
- Hepatitis B surface antigen (HBsAg) and core antibody (anti-HBc) screening
- Hepatitis C antibody screening
