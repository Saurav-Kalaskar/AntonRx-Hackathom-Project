Cigna Endocrinology Medical Policy - Empagliflozin (Jardiance)
Effective Date: March 2025 | Policy Number: CIG-ENDO-2025-JARDIANCE

================================================================================
SECTION 1: Covered Indications
================================================================================

- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with HbA1c above individualized target despite lifestyle modification (ICD-10: E11.9)
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with heart failure risk profile and need for cardiometabolic risk reduction (ICD-10: E11.59)
- CKD risk reduction in Type 2 Diabetes with albuminuria (ICD-10: E11.22)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: Prior Authorization Requirements
================================================================================

Coverage requires documented diagnosis, problem list, recent HbA1c, and baseline renal function. The provider must submit recent blood pressure and volume status assessment.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: Step Therapy Requirements
================================================================================

Step therapy requires trial of metformin and one additional lower-cost oral antihyperglycemic unless contraindicated or not tolerated. Non-preferred requests must include rationale for bypassing formulary alternatives.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: Site of Care Restrictions
================================================================================

Medication is eligible through standard outpatient dispensing with adherence monitoring through the chronic care pathway.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: Dosing and Administration
================================================================================

Recommended dose is 10 mg once daily with optional titration to 25 mg once daily after four weeks when clinically appropriate.
