UnitedHealthcare Pulmonology Medical Policy - Dupilumab (Dupixent)
Effective Date: May 2025 | Policy Number: UHC-PULM-2025-DUPIXENT

================================================================================
SECTION 1: Covered Indications
================================================================================

- Severe asthma with eosinophilic phenotype and frequent exacerbations despite high-intensity controller therapy (ICD-10: J45.50)
- Corticosteroid-dependent asthma where biologic therapy is clinically necessary to reduce oral steroid burden (ICD-10: J45.52)
- Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis refractory to intranasal corticosteroid therapy (ICD-10: J33.9)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: Prior Authorization Requirements
================================================================================

Coverage requires spirometry findings, blood eosinophil level, exacerbation history, and verification of inhaler adherence with proper technique.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: Step Therapy Requirements
================================================================================

Coverage is non-preferred if patient has not completed guideline-based inhaled therapy optimization and specialist evaluation.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: Site of Care Restrictions
================================================================================

First administration is covered in outpatient specialty care; subsequent doses may be administered at home after education and tolerance confirmation.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: Dosing and Administration
================================================================================

Recommended maintenance dosing is 300 mg subcutaneous every two weeks with ongoing assessment of exacerbation reduction and steroid minimization.
