Cigna Dermatology & Inflammatory Disease Policy — Ustekinumab (Stelara)
Effective Date: January 2024 | Policy Number: CDI-2024-STELARA-002

================================================================================
SECTION 1: COVERED INDICATIONS
================================================================================

Ustekinumab (Stelara) is a human monoclonal antibody targeting the p40 subunit of
interleukin-12 (IL-12) and interleukin-23 (IL-23), approved for:
- Plaque Psoriasis (moderate-to-severe): Adults and pediatric patients ≥6 years (ICD-10: L40.0)
- Psoriatic Arthritis: Adults (ICD-10: M07.3)
- Crohn's Disease: Moderately to severely active, adult patients (ICD-10: K50.9)
- Ulcerative Colitis: Moderately to severely active, adult patients (ICD-10: K51.9)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: PRIOR AUTHORIZATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

2.1 PLAQUE PSORIASIS — STEP THERAPY REQUIREMENTS
Cigna requires the following step therapy before approving Stelara for plaque psoriasis:

Step 1 (required, minimum 12-week trial):
- Conventional systemic therapy:
  - Methotrexate (oral, minimum 15 mg/week titrated) OR
  - Cyclosporine (3-5 mg/kg/day) OR
  - Acitretin

Step 2 (required after Step 1 failure for TNF-naive patients):
- At least ONE TNF-alpha inhibitor (adalimumab/Humira preferred brand, OR etanercept):
  - Minimum trial duration: 12 consecutive weeks
  - Documented inadequate response (BSA improvement < 50% from baseline) or intolerance

Stelara may be approved first-line WITHOUT step therapy if:
- Patient has a documented contraindication to methotrexate (hepatic disease, pregnancy risk, significant cytopenias)
- TNF inhibitors are contraindicated (active demyelinating disease, CHF NYHA Class III/IV, history of lymphoma)
- Patient has body surface area (BSA) involvement ≥ 10% with DLQI > 10

2.2 CROHN'S DISEASE — CRITERIA
- Crohn's Disease Activity Index (CDAI) ≥ 220 or Harvey-Bradshaw Index ≥ 8 at baseline
- Patient must have failed or been intolerant to conventional therapy:
  - Corticosteroids (prednisone ≥ 40mg/day for ≥ 4 weeks)
  - Immunomodulators (azathioprine, 6-mercaptopurine, or methotrexate for ≥ 12 weeks)
- For biologic-naive patients: Stelara may be approved if conventional therapy has failed
- For TNF-exposed patients: Stelara is appropriate if patient failed adalimumab or infliximab

2.3 DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
1. Confirmed diagnosis with appropriate ICD-10 code
2. Dermatologist/gastroenterologist/rheumatologist attestation
3. BSA percentage for psoriasis OR CDAI score for Crohn's disease
4. Documentation of all prior therapy trials with dates, doses, and reason for discontinuation
5. TB screening (QuantiFERON or PPD) within 12 months prior to initiation
6. Hepatitis B surface antigen and core antibody testing

================================================================================
SECTION 3: DOSING FOR PRIOR AUTHORIZATION
================================================================================

Plaque Psoriasis / Psoriatic Arthritis:
- ≤ 100 kg: 45 mg SC at weeks 0, 4, then every 12 weeks
- > 100 kg: 90 mg SC at weeks 0, 4, then every 12 weeks

Crohn's Disease / Ulcerative Colitis:
- IV induction dose (weight-based):
  - ≤ 55 kg: 260 mg IV
  - 55–85 kg: 390 mg IV
  - > 85 kg: 520 mg IV
- Maintenance: 90 mg SC every 8 weeks starting week 8

================================================================================
SECTION 4: RENEWAL CRITERIA
================================================================================

- Maintained clinical response: BSA improvement ≥ 50% from baseline for psoriasis, OR CDAI reduction ≥ 100 for Crohn's
- No serious adverse events attributable to Stelara (infections, malignancies, RPLS)
- Annual TB re-screening

================================================================================
SECTION 5: BIOSIMILAR POLICY
================================================================================

Cigna acknowledges FDA-approved biosimilar ustekinumab products. Per Cigna's
biosimilar substitution policy, biosimilar alternatives must be considered and
documented as unavailable or clinically inappropriate before brand Stelara is approved.
Covered biosimilars include: Wezlana (ustekinumab-auub), Otezyla (ustekinumab-aauz).
