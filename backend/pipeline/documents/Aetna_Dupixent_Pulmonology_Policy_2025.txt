Aetna Pulmonology Medical Policy - Dupilumab (Dupixent)
Effective Date: May 2025 | Policy Number: AET-PULM-2025-DUPIXENT

================================================================================
SECTION 1: Covered Indications
================================================================================

- Moderate-to-severe eosinophilic asthma uncontrolled on high-dose inhaled corticosteroid plus long-acting beta agonist (ICD-10: J45.50)
- Oral corticosteroid-dependent asthma requiring steroid-sparing biologic strategy (ICD-10: J45.52)
- Chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps with inadequate response to intranasal corticosteroids (ICD-10: J33.9)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: Prior Authorization Requirements
================================================================================

Provider must submit baseline eosinophil count, exacerbation history, pulmonary function data, and current controller regimen. Documentation of adherence to maintenance inhaler therapy is required.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: Step Therapy Requirements
================================================================================

Step therapy requires optimized inhaled corticosteroid and long-acting bronchodilator therapy for at least twelve weeks unless contraindicated.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: Site of Care Restrictions
================================================================================

Initial dose may be administered in a supervised outpatient setting, with transition to home self-administration when clinically appropriate.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: Dosing and Administration
================================================================================

Typical dosing is 300 mg subcutaneously every two weeks for severe asthma, with alternate dosing schedules permitted by labeled indication.
