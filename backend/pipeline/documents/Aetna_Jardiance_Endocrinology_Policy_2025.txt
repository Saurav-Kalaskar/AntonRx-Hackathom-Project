Aetna Endocrinology Medical Policy - Empagliflozin (Jardiance)
Effective Date: February 2025 | Policy Number: AET-ENDO-2025-JARDIANCE

================================================================================
SECTION 1: Covered Indications
================================================================================

- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with inadequate glycemic control on metformin (ICD-10: E11.9)
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ICD-10: E11.59)
- Chronic Kidney Disease associated with Type 2 Diabetes (ICD-10: E11.22)

================================================================================
SECTION 2: Prior Authorization Requirements
================================================================================

Approval requires documented diagnosis, baseline HbA1c, current medication list, and provider plan for renal monitoring.
Requests must include recent eGFR and confirmation that the patient is not in diabetic ketoacidosis at initiation.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: Step Therapy Requirements
================================================================================

Step therapy requires trial of metformin for at least 90 days unless contraindicated by severe gastrointestinal intolerance or chronic kidney disease stage requiring alternate strategy.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: Site of Care Restrictions
================================================================================

This oral therapy is covered under outpatient pharmacy and ambulatory management programs with no infusion site requirement.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: Dosing and Administration
================================================================================

Initial dose is 10 mg orally once daily and may be increased to 25 mg daily based on clinical response and tolerability.
