Aetna Rheumatology Drug Policy — Adalimumab (Humira) and Biosimilars
Effective Date: January 2024 | Policy Number: ARD-2024-HUMIRA-004

================================================================================
SECTION 1: COVERED INDICATIONS
================================================================================

Adalimumab (Humira) and biosimilar adalimumab products are covered under Aetna's
medical benefit and pharmacy benefit for the following conditions:

- Rheumatoid Arthritis (moderate-to-severe): ICD-10 M05.x, M06.x
- Psoriatic Arthritis: ICD-10 M07.3
- Ankylosing Spondylitis: ICD-10 M45.x
- Plaque Psoriasis (moderate-to-severe): ICD-10 L40.0, BSA ≥ 10%
- Crohn's Disease (moderate-to-severe): ICD-10 K50.x
- Ulcerative Colitis (moderate-to-severe): ICD-10 K51.x
- Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis: ICD-10 M08.0x, patients ≥ 2 years

================================================================================
SECTION 2: RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS — PRIOR AUTHORIZATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

2.1 DISEASE ACTIVITY THRESHOLDS (must meet at least ONE):
- Clinical Disease Activity Index (CDAI) > 22 (high disease activity)
- DAS28-CRP > 5.1
- ≥ 6 tender joints AND ≥ 6 swollen joints
- Elevated inflammatory markers: CRP > 2x ULN or ESR > 50 mm/hr

2.2 STEP THERAPY REQUIREMENTS
Step 1 — Required Conventional DMARD Trial (minimum 3 months at therapeutic dose):
  Primary option: Methotrexate (MTX)
    - Must reach minimum 15 mg/week; target 20-25 mg/week
    - Folic acid supplementation 1 mg/day required
    - If MTX is contraindicated or not tolerated after 8+ weeks:
      Acceptable alternatives: Leflunomide (10-20 mg/day) OR Hydroxychloroquine + Sulfasalazine combination

Step 2 — Inadequate Response Documentation:
  - Physician-attested inadequate clinical response after ≥ 12 weeks at therapeutic MTX dose
  - OR documented intolerance: hepatotoxicity (ALT/AST > 3x ULN), cytopenias, GI intolerance unmanageable with antiemetics, pulmonary toxicity

Step 3 — Qualifying for Adalimumab/Biosimilar Approval:
  - Documented Step 1 DMARD trial completion AND failure
  - Disease activity confirmed at screening visit
  - No active/serious infection including TB (see Section 5)
  - Negative pregnancy test (for women of childbearing potential)

2.3 STEP THERAPY EXCEPTION — BYPASS CRITERIA
PA may be approved for adalimumab WITHOUT DMARD step therapy if:
- Rapid progressive erosive joint disease on X-ray within past 6 months
- Extra-articular manifestations (serositis, vasculitis, Felty syndrome)
- CDAI > 38 at presentation despite any oral therapy
- Physician documents three or more failed DMARDs previously

================================================================================
SECTION 3: PLAQUE PSORIASIS — PRIOR AUTHORIZATION CRITERIA
================================================================================

3.1 SEVERITY THRESHOLD (must meet):
- Body Surface Area (BSA) ≥ 10% OR
- Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI) score ≥ 12 OR
- DLQI (Dermatology Life Quality Index) > 10
- Disease involving hands, feet, face, or genitalia (even if BSA < 10%)

3.2 STEP THERAPY FOR PSORIASIS
Step 1 — Topical therapy (minimum 3 months):
  - High-potency corticosteroid (e.g., clobetasol 0.05%) AND calcipotriene combination
  - Plus UV phototherapy (NB-UVB or PUVA) if access available — minimum 24 sessions

Step 2 — Systemic conventional therapy (minimum 12 weeks each, at therapeutic dose):
  At least ONE of the following:
  - Methotrexate (oral or SC, 15-25 mg/week)
  - Cyclosporine (3-5 mg/kg/day; maximum 12-week continuous course due to nephrotoxicity)
  - Acitretin (25-50 mg/day)

Adalimumab is approved as THIRD-LINE if Steps 1 and 2 are documented failures.
EXCEPTION: Adalimumab may be approved second-line if ALL systemic conventional therapies are contraindicated.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: DOSING AND BENEFIT ADMINISTRATION
================================================================================

Standard Dosing — Rheumatoid Arthritis:
- 40 mg SC every other week (Q2W)
- For inadequate response on Q2W: 40 mg SC weekly may be authorized

Standard Dosing — Plaque Psoriasis:
- Initial: 80 mg SC (Day 1)
- 40 mg at Day 8
- Then 40 mg SC every other week

Standard Dosing — Crohn's Disease / Ulcerative Colitis:
- Induction: 160 mg SC at Week 0 (split over 2 days), 80 mg at Week 2
- Maintenance: 40 mg SC every other week starting Week 4

Benefit Administration:
- Aetna covers adalimumab biosimilars under both pharmacy (self-injectable) and
  medical benefit (physician-administered) based on site of care
- Citrate-free biosimilar formulations preferred for patient comfort

================================================================================
SECTION 5: TUBERCULOSIS AND INFECTION SCREENING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

MANDATORY before initiating adalimumab or biosimilar:

TB Screening:
- IGRA (QuantiFERON-TB Gold Plus) preferred; TST (Mantoux) acceptable
- Positive IGRA/TST: Confirm with chest X-ray; initiate 9 months isoniazid prophylaxis and
  delay adalimumab until 1 month of isoniazid completed minimum

Hepatitis Screening:
- HBsAg, anti-HBc total, anti-HBs (quantitative)
- Active HBV: Adalimumab contraindicated; refer to hepatology for antiviral management
- Resolved HBV (anti-HBc positive, HBsAg negative): Prophylactic antiviral (entecavir or tenofovir)
  required during treatment and 12 months after discontinuation
- HCV: Screen with anti-HCV antibody; active HCV is NOT an absolute contraindication but
  requires hepatology co-management

===============================================================================
SECTION 6: RENEWAL CRITERIA (12-MONTH REVIEW)
===============================================================================

Clinical response must be documented at each renewal using standardized scales:
- RA: CDAI ≤ 10 (low disease activity) or ≥ 50% reduction from baseline
- Psoriasis: PASI improvement ≥ 75% (PASI 75) from baseline OR BSA < 3%
- Crohn's: CDAI < 150 (remission) or ≥ 100-point reduction from baseline
- UC: Mayo score ≤ 2 (remission) or partial Mayo score reduction ≥ 3 points

Failure to demonstrate clinical response at 6-month review (for RA) or annual review
will result in non-renewal. Physician may appeal with clinical justification.
